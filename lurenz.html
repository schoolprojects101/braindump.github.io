A Bite of Buwagsong: Unveiling the Mighty Bakasi at Entoy's Bakasihan

By: Lurenz B. Arain

Welcome to a flavorful expedition through the charming fishing community of Buwagsong in Cordova, where the culinary spotlight shines on the esteemed bakasi. In the midst of Cordova's tidal flats, characterized by mud and grass, the bakasi thrives abundantly, becoming a beloved symbol in the local cuisine.

At the heart of this culinary adventure lies Entoy's Bakasihan, a culinary haven with a legacy spanning over three decades. What began as a humble passion for cooking transformed into a thriving restaurant, thanks to the encouragement of friends who recognized the culinary prowess of Entoy. Thus, Entoy's Bakasihan etched its place in Cordova's gastronomic history.

A standout dish in Entoy's culinary repertoire is the linarang nga bakasi, a masterpiece where eel is sautéed and then simmered in a tantalizing blend of soy sauce, black beans, and sambag (tamarind). This dish, often likened to the Asian tom yum, has garnered a devoted following, particularly among men who believe in the legendary aphrodisiac properties of the eel. During my visit, the majority of diners were men, including taxi drivers, salesmen, and intrigued Korean and Vietnamese tourists.

Entoy's menu also boasts the piniritong bakasi, or fried eel—a crispy delight that captivates even the most discerning palates. The eels, masterfully deep-fried to perfection, offer a crunchy texture, complemented by a dipping sauce crafted with vinegar, soy sauce, and a generous amount of chili. Be prepared for a subtle bitter aftertaste, a characteristic of the eel remaining ungutted. Given the small size of bakasi, typically measuring 8-12 inches, the process is meticulous, but it adds an intriguing layer to the culinary experience. Notably, the bile of the eel is believed to possess medicinal properties, adding another dimension to the allure of this unique dish.

For those embarking on a bakasi culinary journey for the first time, I recommend starting with the smaller ones and gradually progressing in size. Each bite unveils the rich culinary heritage of Cordova, providing a glimpse into the bold flavors that have transformed bakasi into a symbol of this vibrant fishing community.

As you savor the linarang nga bakasi and piniritong bakasi, you are not just indulging in a meal but participating in the narrative of Cordova's rich history. Each dish tells a tale of resilience, community, and the symbiotic relationship between the people of Cordova and the abundant resources of the tidal flats.

Join me in this culinary odyssey, where we explore the essence of Cordova through the distinctive and delectable flavors of bakasi. In each bite, we celebrate not just a local delicacy but a cultural legacy that continues to thrive, making Entoy's Bakasihan a culinary beacon in the heart of Buwagsong, Cordova.

The transition from daylight to dusk is a spectacle in itself, casting a warm glow over the bamboo cottages and creating an ambiance that is both romantic and captivating.
